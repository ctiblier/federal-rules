A witness may not testify to a matter unless evidence is introduced sufficient to support a finding that the witness has personal knowledge of the matter. Evidence to prove personal knowledge may, but need not, consist of the witnessâ€™ own testimony. This rule is subject to the provisions of rule 703, relating to opinion testimony by expert witnesses.
